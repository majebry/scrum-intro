# Scrum Infograph
### Scrum Roles ![alt text](images/roles.png)
#### Scrum Master A scrum master is usually a member of the development team. It's a conflict of interest for a scrum master to be the manager.
#### Scrum Master Main Responsibilities - Hosts the rituals - Maintains the artifacts - Assists to remove blockers - Protects the team - Coaches the team about Scrum - Represents the needs of scrum to the organization - Works with the product owner on writing stories
#### Product Owner One of the most critical duties of a product owner is clarifying the needs of the customer, developing a backlog of product features to work on, and writing clear stories that communicate the full expectations for the product, while meeting the standards of the team.
#### Product Owner Main Responsibilities - Meets regularly with customers - Writes user stories - Works directly with designers - Gets technical approval from developers
#### Scrum Team Member Scrum Development Team is a cross-functional team that consists of individuals coming from different backgrounds.
#### Scrum Team Member Main Responsibilities - Writes code - Participates in the rituals - Works with the scrum master to remove blockers
##### Pair Programming ![alt text](images/pair-programming.jpg)
##### Pair Programming Pair programming is the practice of two people share a single monitor, and trade responsibility for entering code and keeping track of what needs to happen next. A pair should be talking with each other constantly as they work, and neither member of a pair should allow distractions such as email to interfere with the process.
#### Scrum Team Members - The team has the right to reject stories that are not fully clear - The team is expected to push back against any attempts to sacrifice quality for the sake of speed - Every member of the team is also responsible for maintaining the scrum process
#### Team Resources - Designers - QA Engineers
##### Designers Designers have become elemental in all phases of product development, from initial problem definition through wireframing, creating style guides, building graphical assets, validating visual consistency, and doing user experience testing. Designers work closely with product owners during the development of new stories.
##### QA Engineers Ideally, QA should be considered part of the team's definition of "done" for any story. This means that QA engineers should start writing the tests for a story at the same time that developers are creating the code.
#### Summary - A scrum master hosts the rituals, supports the team, and evangelizes the scrum process, both inside and outside of scrum. - A product owner maintains the vision and direction of the product, and is responsible for interacting with the customer. - A development team is made up of engineers who organize themselves to estimate and do the work to build the product.
### Scrum Rituals Ritual is a face-to-face gathering. ![alt text](images/rituals.png)
##### Not Another Meeting Each ritual: - Has a specific objective - Addresses a particular audionce - Has a defined timeframes - Has predictable outcomes
### Sprint Word Definition ![alt text](images/sprint.png)
##### Sprint Length From 1 to 4 weeks. It's important to stick with the choosed length for serveral sprints.
#### Sprint Planning Ritual Setting agenda for the coming sprint
##### Sprint Planning | Time Box Suggestion: 4 hours for each couple of weeks sprint.
#### Sprint Planning | Preparation The Product Owner is responsible of the sprint planning content, he/she works with designers, customers, team members and the scrum master to create backlog of the highest piority stories.
#### Sprint Planning | Agenda ###### 1) Product Owner Introduce The Stories - Every team member must participate actively in the sprint planning - Team may challange some stories - Team may present stories related to the code
#### Sprint Planning | Agenda ###### 2) Story Estimation Suggestion: 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 20 - The goal is to know how many points team can complete in a sprint - Every team member shoud agree to the estimation
#### Sprint Planning | Agenda ###### 3) Commiting to Sprint Backlog - The scrum master should make sure the team's commitiment is realistic. Therefore, every Team member should have a good sense of what they have to do.
#### Daily Stand-up Ritual An opportunity to get a picture of what other mates are working on, and the status of the iteration or to speak out any Blockers. Only Team Members are allowed to present, anybody in the company may attend.
#### Daily Stand-up | Time Box Up to 15 mins.
#### Daily Stand-up | Preparation Each Team Member shoud think before to present the work done in a clear and understandable way , and to answer the three questions
##### The Three Questions - What's done since the last stand-up? - What they plan to do until the next one? - Any Blockers?
#### Sprint Demo Ritual - The team demonstrates the stories they done - Product owner accepts or rejects stories - Guests are welcomed to observe the current state of the project - Team learns how much work they can do in a sprint
#### Sprint Demo | Time Box - May take up to half a day in 2 week sprints
#### Sprint Demo | Preparation Engineers get with the Scrum Master and Product Owner to prepare the stories that are going to be preseneted
##### Sprint Retrospective * Everyone will be asked: - What went well? - What didn't? - What actions the group will commit to in order to improve? * Development Team, Product Owner and Scrum Master are expected to participate * Guests are not so welcomed here :|
###### The Prime Directive "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand."
##### Sprint Retrospective | TimeBox It's important for the retro ritual to have enough time for its objective, half a day in 2 weeks sprints is a valid suggetions.
##### Sprint Retrospective | Preperation Every team member should have at least 1 answer for each of the three retro questions.
### Scrum Artifacts "Artifacts" are the tools of scrum that allow teams to manage their scrum process.
### Scrum Artifacts - Story - Product Backlog - Sprint Backlog - Scrum Board - Definition of "done" - Sprint Goal - ...
### Stories - Stories are how the "product owner" communicates to the development team what needs to be developed. - A story should capture a complete (small) slice of functionality. - Every story should add to the value of the product, - Stories Are not Technical Specifications!
### Stories The responsibility for writing the stories belongs to the product owner. Writing stories that capture the full intention and expectations of the client is a skill that may take time to develop, but it's critical to an effective scrum process. A good "scrum master" should be able to help coach the product owner in how to write stories that are the right size and express the appropriate "acceptance criteria".
#### Story Formula Name: brief understandable feature name - As a *type of user* - I want to *behavior* - so that *justification for the behavior* Acceptance Criteria: - Given a *defined state* - when a *set of conditions or events* - then a *consistent and testable result*
#### What makes for a good story? A good story is short and straightforward. Many scrum teams follow the convention that a story should be able to be written on one side of an index card,
#### Story Example Name: Rating Gallery Images - As a gallery viewer - I want to rate the images - so that I can track and rank the images I've rated
#### Story Example Acceptance Criteria: - Given a logged-in user viewing a gallery - when the user clicks a star in a rating widget for an image - then the value of that star should be recorded as that user's rating for that image
#### Story Real-life Example Name: View Subscription Plans - As a User - I want to view Subscription Packages - So that I can compare between them and choose the appropriate package for me
#### Story Example Acceptance Criteria: - Given a user viewing the app - When the user chooses to view packages - then a list of package specifications should appear to them
### Stories When a story is presented at sprint planning, the engineers should verify that all of the necessary assets and information they'll need have been captured and are available. The actual work to be done in the upcoming sprint is defined by the description on the story card and the dialogue it inspires between the product owner and the team.
### Product Backlog ![alt text](images/backlog.png)
### Product Backlog Stories for the development team emerge from the product owner's product backlog. A product backlog keeps track of all the input the product owner has about the direction of the product from the client, along with the research, experience testing, design, and engineering feedback the product owner has gathered.
### Sprint Backlog ![alt text](images/sprint-backlog.png)
### Sprint Backlog The sprint backlog is the set of developer stories that the team has committed to work on during the current sprint. A sprint backlog is created as part of the sprint planning ritual, and reflects the shared understanding of the team, the product owner, and the scrum master of what everybody will be working on for the coming sprint.
### Definition of Done ![alt text](images/done.png)
### Definition of Done It's vital for a team to have a shared idea of what done actually means for itself. Teams should create a written definition of done which they maintain as a checklist for every story they work on.
#### Definition of Done A definition of done is something the team should come up with together. It may be created during the first sprint planning, and iterated on during sprint retrospectives. A team's definition of done may involve radically over time, as the team realizes during retrospectives what aspects of the process may need improvement.
### Sprint Goal - The Sprint Goal is an objective set for the Sprint. - Sprint Goals should be specific and measurable.
